Physics Overview

Fundamental

Physics is the natural science that studies matter, its motion and behavior through space and time, and the related entities of energy and force. Physics is one of the most fundamental scientific disciplines, and its main goal is to understand how the universe behaves.

Major Branches of Physics

  • Classical Mechanics: Study of motion and forces
  • Quantum Mechanics: Behavior of matter at atomic and subatomic levels
  • Thermodynamics: Heat, temperature, and energy transfer
  • Electromagnetism: Electric and magnetic phenomena
  • Relativity: Space, time, and gravity

Classical Mechanics

Intermediate

Classical mechanics is a physical theory describing the motion of macroscopic objects, from projectiles to parts of machinery, and astronomical objects, such as spacecraft, planets, stars and galaxies.

Key Concepts

  • Newton's Laws of Motion: The foundation of classical mechanics
  • Energy and Work: Kinetic and potential energy
  • Momentum: Conservation of momentum
  • Rotational Motion: Angular velocity and acceleration
  • Gravitation: Universal law of gravitation
